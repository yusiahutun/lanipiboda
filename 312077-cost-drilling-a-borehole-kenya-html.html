Cost of drilling a borehole in Kenya

March 2024 Â· 5 minute read

In Kenya, boreholes have been an alternative source of water in areas where there is no ready supply of fresh water. The boreholes were initially dug manually until recently when drilling machines were developed. So, what is the cost of drilling a borehole in Kenya? Is it a million shillings, two million or just a lot of money? Well, the cost differs depending on the company you hire. One thing though, the price discrepancies are not that huge. So what is the actual cost of drilling a borehole in Kenya?

Cost of drilling a borehole in Kenya is arrived at after calculating the cost of services from different stakeholders, beginning with environmental impact assessors, to NEMA certification and the actual drilling. The scale of your borehole is also a major determining factor when arriving at the cost of drilling a borehole. This article seeks to inform you about all you need to know about drilling a borehole.

Factors that influence the costing

Most real estate developers, landlords and industrialists opt for drilling as a better option for availing water into their premises. This solves the question of unreliable water supply and in turn, attracts a huge number of tenants into the residential apartments. However, the cost of drilling will differ depending on the following factors:

1. Surveys

Surveys are done to determine the suitability of the site as a good water source.

2. The physical distance of the borehole drilling companies

When a company is located far from the site, the cost of drilling might go high because of logistical costs.

3. A company's pricing policy

Companies differ in pricing policies. Some charge per cubic meter while others charge the process as a whole. These results to different costs at the end of the day.

4. Geological characteristics of the site

If the site is difficult to drill the cost will be higher because more power will be needed to get through the ground.

5. The extent of the hole

The depth and width of the hole will determine the amount of material used. It will also determine the pump to be used, thus increasing the cost.

6. The pump used

The choice of the pump to be used depends on the type of hole, and geological characteristics of the site, which, in turn, affects the cost of drilling.

7. Licensing requirements

Some licensing requirements will need to be paid for, thus increasing the cost of drilling.

Process of drilling

The process of drilling a borehole in its entirety might cost between Ksh 1.2 million and Ksh 3 million. This is in spite of the many fears people have on matters cost and complexity of the process.

The process of drilling a borehole starts with a hydro-geological survey. This survey is done to determine the groundwater levels and hydraulic traits of the site. It should only be done by a qualified and certified geologist. After the geologist pronounces the groundwater potential as good, the drilling company seeks the authorization to continue drilling from the Water Resource Management Authority (WRMA).

After WRMA has given the company the go-ahead, an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) is done to determine the suitability of the project and its potential effects on the environment. If the National Environmental Management Authority (NEMA), is satisfied with the EIA findings, it issues licenses to the company to proceed to drill. With these, the company may proceed with the drilling process. However, in some cases, the company might need a no-objection letter from the local water suppliers.

Drilling contractors

There are a number of drilling contractors in Kenya, who deal in shallow well drilling as well as other forms of water drilling in Kenya. The cost to dig a well also differs depending on the contractor hired. Here are some of the best drilling contractors in Kenya.

Apex boreholes & engineering company

Apex boreholes is a Nairobi based drilling company that serves the larger East African community. This company is involved in, Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA), hydrological surveys, borehole drilling, and equipping (installation of pumps). This company is well equipped and staffed to serve its market well. It has three pump testing units, four rigs, five geologists, four engineers, five terameters for the surveys, and more than ten support staff. They charge an average of Ksh 2 million for their service.

Davis & Shirtliff

This company has been serving this niche market since 1964. It is the number one supplier of water-related equipment in East Africa. It provides the market with water pumps, boreholes, swimming pools, water treatment, generators and solar equipment.
