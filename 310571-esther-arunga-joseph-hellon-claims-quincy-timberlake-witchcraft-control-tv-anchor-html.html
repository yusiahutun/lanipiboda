Esther Arunga: Joseph Hellon claims Quincy Timberlake used witchcraft to control ex-TV anchor

June 2024 · 3 minute read

- Joseph Hellon was a close friend of Esther Arunga until the time she got married to Quincy

- Hellon claimed they parted ways with Arunga after he realised Quincy was a criminal running a theft syndicate in Hurlingham

- He alleged Quincy had told him he joined his church to reform from witchcraft

- Arunga is set for sentencing over murder of her son Sinclair Timberlake on Thursday, July 18

Founder of the defunct Finger of God Ministry, Joseph Hellon, has claimed Quincy Timberlake used witchcraft to manipulate former television anchor Esther Arunga.

According to Hellon, when Quincy joined the church he informed him that he was using black magic and wanted to reform.

Speaking to NTV, Hellon claimed Arunga's husband was cyber-criminal conning people on the internet and had even confronted him over the theft syndicate he was running in Hurlingham, Nairobi.

"Quincy was not only manipulative but he used witchcraft...He told me before he joined the church that he was using black magic and he wanted to practice witchcraft and he wanted to reform," said Hellon.

The jazz maestro further said that the moment he told Arunga about Quincy's underworld affairs, she was not happy with him and that broke their friendship.

The former news anchor's story resurfaced in media after she admitted to giving Australian police wrong information on what led to the death of her three-year-old son, Sinclair Timberlake in June 2014.

Arunga's son died at their family home in Kallangur in North of Brisbane, Australia, after suffering fatal injuries.

Her husband was charged with the murder of the minor soon after the incident.

Arunga found herself in trouble as she was also charged with being an accessory to her son’s murder, just nine days after her husband was charged.

Speaking in court on Monday, July 15, the former TV girl pleaded guilty to being an accessory to the murder saying she had lied about it and instead confessed her husband killed their son.

However, Quincy's mother Rosemeg Wambita dismissed Arunga's testimony saying her son was innocent and that Arunga would have dumped him long time ago if he was a murderer.

Arunga is set for sentencing on Thursday, July 18.
