A Florida woman has been arrested for child neglect for keeping her 11-year-old son in a $300-a-night hotel room with her while she engaged in sex with two men - one of whom died in the bed while the child was nearby. 

Amy Kemper, 32, and her son checked into the Hammock Beach Golf Resort & Spa in Palm Coast, Florida, on Saturday night.

Police say she went there to take drugs and have sex with two older men. 

On Sunday morning, she called 911 in a panic to report that one of the men wasn't breathing. He was later pronounced dead. 

The man's cause of death has not been revealed, but details of the night were disclosed by Sheriff Rick Staly who said that Kemper and her son went to the resort 'in the middle of the night so she could engage in narcotic use and sexual activities.' 

Amy Kemper was arrested and charged with Child Neglect Without Great Bodily Harm and taken to the Sheriff Perry Hall Inmate Detention Facility. She is being held on a $15,000 bond

In 2015, Kemper was charged with a misdemeanor for marijuana possession and was released on the same day. In 2019, she was charged with another misdemeanor, this time for domestic battery, and was released the following day

Kemper booked a $300-a-night room at the Hammock Beach Golf Resort & Spa for her, her  11-year-old son and two unidentified men

The Sheriff's office confirmed that Kemper's son was in the room the entire time. 

According to an arrest report from the Flagler County Sheriff's Office, Kemper said she thought the dead male was sleeping and reportedly heard him 'snoring unusually'.

Kemper was charged with Child Neglect Without Great Bodily Harm and taken to the Sheriff Perry Hall Inmate Detention Facility. She is being held on a $15,000 bond. 

Her son was taken by The Florida Department of Children and Families while his mom was taken away. 

The two men involved have not been identified and it is unclear who the father of the boy is. It is also unclear as to why her child was in the room with her at the time of the incident.
