10 Similar Shows to 'Stranger Things'

August 2024 Â· 5 minute read
1

I Am Not Okay With This

Courtesy of Netflix

This Netflix series also follows an awkward teenage girl with supernatural powers. 17-year-old Sydney (Sophia Lillis) lives in a Rust Belt town, where she's mourning her father's recent death and becoming distant from her best friend. She soon makes friends with Stanley (Wyatt Olef), a friendly weirdo who becomes her love interest and encouraging sidekick as her powers start to emerge. Though the show's more teen-angst drama then mystery tour, there's also plenty of '80s references to soothe your pop culture nostalgia.

Watch on Netflix

2

Dark

Julia Terjung/Netflix

Dark is Netflix's first-ever German-language show, and it follows four families living a town where many children are mysteriously disappearing. That's the most we can say about this thriller without getting into spoiler territory, as the surprises in this show are best experienced cold. For those willing to go in blind, the three-season series' extremely loyal cult-following and critic comparisons to Stranger Things are more than enough for a leap of faith.

Watch on Netflix

3

The OA

JoJo Whilden/Netflix

The cancellation of this trippy sci-fi series prompted a major, years-long fan backlash for a reason. Its central mystery, following a missing woman who reemerges after seven years and gathers a group of strangers to help her get to another dimension, grows over two seasons to involve a kidnapping scientist, the Russian mob, angels, and a telepathic octopus. Also, Zendaya shows up in a pre-Euphoria dramatic role in season 2.

Watch on Netflix

4

Silverpoint

This new CBBC series takes the Scooby-Doo spirit of Stranger Things' teen investigators and transplants it to a summer camp. A group of campers looking into four teenagers who disappeared in the woods 23 years age stumble upon a paranormal phenomenon. Its mood is also lighter than ST, for anyone who has nightmares thinking about the Upside Down.

Watch on Hulu

5

The Society

Courtesy of Netflix

This Netflix teen series offers a more suburban take on Yellowjackets or Return of the Flies. Upon returning home from an extended camping trip, a group of high school students discover that all of their town's adults are gone, and miles of uncharted forest have suddenly appeared around the city. Isolated, the kids have to come up with some order system and learn to take care of each other, while also investigating what happened to the adults.

Watch on Netflix

6

Paradise (Paraiso)

HBO Max

Another international spin on teen sci-fi, this Spanish series follows a group of teens living in coastal town Valencia in the '90s. When three 15-year-old girls have mysteriously disappeared from a nightclub, one of the girls' younger brother, Javi, teams up with his two best friends and the school bully to find them. The kids soon discover that the girls were taken by something not of this world.

Watch on HBO Max

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

Locke & Key

Christos Kalohoridis//Netflix

At the start of this magical series, the three siblings of the Locke family move into their ancestral New England home, Keyhouse, following their father's murder. There they discover magical keys with different powers, as well as a demon who wants to use the keys for itself. The trio has to figure out where the keys came from and send the demon back wherever it came from.

Watch on Netflix

8

The Umbrella Academy

Courtesy of Netflix

This Netflix hit about superhero siblings who are dysfunctional as they are powerful is a fun watch for anyone still wondering about Eleven's lab "siblings." Based on Gerard Way's comic series, the seven members of the Umbrella Academy were all born on the same day to women who weren't pregnant that morning. They were then adopted and trained by a masochistic billionaire, leaving the grown kids with scars they have to deal with while also stopping the end of the world.

Watch on Netflix

9

Marvel's Runaways

Paul Sarkis/Hulu

The parents of Stranger Things are blissfully unaware of the monsters from the Upside Down, but what if they were actually the villains of the story? This adaptation of the comic series follows a group of super-powered teens who discover their parents carrying out what looks like a human sacrifice, and team together to figure out the adults' secrets. The show is also from Josh Schwartz and Stephanie Savage, so if you've ever wanted the tenor of The O.C. but with superheroes, Runaways is a must-watch.

Watch on Disney+

10

Castle Rock

Hulu

The Duffer Brothers' initial premise for Stranger Things was "What if Steven Spielberg directed a Stephen King book?" For fans who want to go all in on the legendary horror novelist's influence, this psychological-horror anthology is set within the world of King's novels, with settings like the Shawshank Penitentiary and characters like Annie Wilkes taking center stage. Even if you won't recognize the King Easter Eggs, there's the cast, including Melanie Lynskey, Andre Holland, Lizzy Caplan, and Sissy Spacek.

Watch on Hulu
