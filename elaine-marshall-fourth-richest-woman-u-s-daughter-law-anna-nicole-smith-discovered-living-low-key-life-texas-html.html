You would think it would be a challenge to stay under the radar with a fortune of $12.7 billion - especially as the daughter-in-law of famous octogenarian lover, Anna Nicole Smith.

Yet Elaine Marshall, 70, has only just been revealed as the fourth richest woman in the United States after eluding international wealth rankings and enjoying a quiet life in Dallas, Texas.

Peter Newcomb, the editor of Bloomberg News's Billionaire Index, said the reason for her being missed off lists is simply that 'she's extremely low-key'.

Under the radar: Elaine Marshall has been named as the fourth richest woman in the U.S. after inheriting $12.7 billion worth of shares from her husband, E. Pierce Marshall, pictured right in 2001

It is a life in stark contrast with that of Marshall's late mother-in-law, Anna Nicole Smith, who was 26 years her junior. Smith was just 26 when she married oil tycoon J. Howard Marshall, 89, in 1994.

Elaine also married into the Marshall family's wealth through her nuptials to J. Marshall's son, E. Pierce Marshall. When he died aged 67 in 2006, she inherited his fortune and shares.

She managed to keep her bank balance quiet until Bloomberg questioned who owned a 15 per cent minority stake in Koch Industries, the second-largest company in the country.

The majority of the company, which makes sales of $110 billion a year, is owned by the Koch brothers, Charles and David, who are famous for pouring money into conservative causes.

Mother-in-law: Pierce Marshall's father was oil tycoon J. Howard Marshall who famously married former Playmate of the year Anna Nicole Smith, both pictured, in 1994 when he was 89 and she was 26

In the public eye: Smith unsuccessfully bid for a share of her husband's fortune in court after he died in 1995

The partnership between the Koch and Marshall families began in 1959, when founder Fred C. Koch acquired a stake in a Minnesota-based refinery co-founded by Howard Marshall, for $5 million.

A decade later, Koch's son Charles offered to swap the rest of Marshall's stake in the refinery for Koch Industries shares and in 1974, J. Howard Marshall passed on these to his sons.

The eye-watering figure puts Elaine Marshall only behind two Walmart heiresses, Alice and Christy Walton, and Jacqueline Mars, who inherited the candy company wealth.

Her two sons have remained in the family trade. Preston Marshall, who lives in Houston, is the president of oil exploration company MarOpCo. A second son, E. Pierce Marshall Jr., a trained lawyer, serves as senior vice president to the company.

In 2010, all three were listed as participants at a secret Koch event in at the Rancho Las Palmas Resort in Rancho Mirage, California, where speakers met to address 'the threats to American enterprise' and how to build on prosperity.

The event, which featured cocktail receptions and gondola rides, was listed as confidential, and participants were instructed not to make copious notes or write about it on social media.

Wealth: Elaine Marshall's fortune comes from a 15 per cent minority stake in Koch Industries, run by brothers David, left, and Charles Koch. The shares have been passed through the Marshall family

The Marshalls desire to stay in the shadows is in sharp contrast with her father-in-law, who garnered international attention when he married former stripper and Playboy model Anna Nicole Smith.

Following his death the following year, his son and Smith wrangled in court over whether she would inherit any of the family fortune.

Smith, who filed for bankruptcy in 1996, claimed during proceedings that her late husband had intended to set up a trust in her benefit, but had died before given the chance.

They had agreed the trust would have totalled half of the increased value of Marshall's assets during their marriage, and Smith accused Pierce Marshall of interfering with the trust's creation.

In 2000, a bankruptcy court in Los Angeles awarded Smith $475 million in damages, agreeing Pierce Marshall had deprived her of her expected gift from her late husband.

Rich: Her wealth puts her behind Jacqueline Badger Mars, the third richest woman in the country

Topping the list: Walmart heiress Alice Walton, left, has a fortune of $27.8 billion, while fellow heiress Christy Walton is the country's richest woman with $30.2 billion

Pierce Marshall appealed the decision and in 2002, the District Court lowered the bankruptcy court’s verdict to $89 million - yet in 2001, a probate court ruled she had no valid claim to the estate.

She never received a cent from the family fortune and died of a drug overdose at age 39 in a Florida hotel in 2007, leaving behind a young daughter from a later relationship.

A representative for Elaine Marshall declined to comment on Bloomberg's revelation of her wealth.

'Mrs. Marshall does not own any Koch stock in her name,' David Margulies told Bloomberg. 'The late E. Pierce Marshall and J. Howard Marshall II consistently stated that their family’s assets were vastly overvalued.'

Bloomberg started its Billionaire Index in March by ranking 20 wealthy individuals. It is updated daily and now includes 100 people, they said.
