FAQs

Q: How do I reach Loch Coruisk?

A: The most common way to reach Loch Coruisk is by taking a boat from Elgol, a small village on the Isle of Skye. Boats run daily during the summer months, offering visitors a scenic and memorable journey through the waters of Loch Scavaig.

Q: Can I hike to Loch Coruisk?

A: Yes, you can hike to Loch Coruisk from various points on the Isle of Skye. The hike can be challenging and requires proper planning and preparation, as it involves traversing steep and rocky terrain. It is recommended for experienced hikers only.

Q: What is the best time to visit Loch Coruisk?

A: The best time to visit Loch Coruisk is during the summer months, between April and September, when the weather is milder and the days are longer. This allows for better boat accessibility and more time to explore the area.

Q: Are there any accommodations near Loch Coruisk?

A: As Loch Coruisk is in a remote location, there are no accommodations directly nearby. However, there are several options for lodging in nearby towns such as Broadford and Portree, which are a short drive away from the boat launch in Elgol.

Q: Can I go fishing in Loch Coruisk?

A: Yes, fishing is allowed in Loch Coruisk, but you will need to obtain a permit beforehand. The loch is home to trout and salmon, providing anglers with a unique and exciting fishing experience.
