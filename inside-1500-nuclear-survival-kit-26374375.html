Inside 1,500 nuclear survival kit with everything family needs to 'live for 72 hours'

April 2024 · 2 minute read

A group of ex-military and police personnel have designed a nuclear survival kit that could help a family of four survive for the first 72-hours after disaster strikes.

Urban Preppers UK is a company run by former army and police staff who primarily deal in goods for surviving in the great outdoors.

However, as World War Three fears increase due to the Russia-Ukraine war - the fear of Vladimir Putin's nuclear weapons has also never been so high.

That's why the company has launched a three-day kit that is suitable for a family of four should such an attack occur.

The pack contains a rucksack, military-grade gas masks with drinking and gas filters, personal hygiene essentials, first aid kit, surgical masks, safety goggles, water bottles, food and water rations, protective clothing for the outdoors, a shovel, rope, matches and gorilla tape.

And while some may say there is no price on the safety of yourself and your loved ones - this shop managed to find one as the kits retail for £1,549.95 on the Urban Preppers website.

Promoting the bags on Facebook, they said: "An act of war is not the only threat from man. Dams burst, power plants go down, pipelines explode, oil spills occur, and other man-made structures and facilities can fail, resulting in disaster.

"Outbreaks of sickness and disease, pandemics could also warrant an evacuation. Our 72-Hour Emergency Kits are packed with survival essentials and is an invaluable and priceless resource.

"In our unstable and unpredictable world economy, we would be foolish to think there is no chance of a terrorist or military attack from forces domestic or foreign that could possibly force us to evacuate our own home without a moment's notice."

Responding to a comment on their post, the company claim they are donating some of the proceeds to supply medical equipment to Ukraine.
