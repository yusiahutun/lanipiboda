She's been going through one of the toughest times of her life, and it seems former Home and Away star Rhiannon Fish is missing the comfort of home.

The 23-year-old actress is in Calgary, Canada after the sudden loss of her older sister Corinne Fish at just 34 years of age.

After sharing the news and her grief with fans on Saturday after the burial, Fish seems to be missing her boyfriend of two years who remained behind in Australia.

On Monday, she posted a picture to her 212,905 followers on Instagram of pop/rock singer Reece Mastin cuddling their two cats.

'From where I'd rather be,' she wrote in a caption underneath the photo.

Her fans took to the comments section to inundate her with messages of support, telling her to 'stay strong' and 'be brave' through the difficult time.

Over the weekend Fish took to Instagram to share her feelings over the tragedy, posting a loving throwback snapshot of herself and her beloved sister 'Cory'.

'You were my role model, my hero and without a doubt, the best big sister anyone could ever wish for,' the caption read.

'I love you. Forever and always. - Your first little sister.'

The snap followed a series of other pictures uploaded by the actress in memory of Cory, whose life was celebrated in a service at a chapel in Calgary, Canada, where the Fish family are from.

Clearly sharing a deep bond, the sisters both grew in Calgary, Canada, before heading Australia - where Corinne lived in Melbourne for three years and Rhiannon eventually settled, finding fame playing girl-next-door April Scott on Home and Away.
