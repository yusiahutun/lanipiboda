The Duchess of Sussex wears green dress during Australia royal tour

August 2024 Â· 2 minute read

Pool/Samir Hussein//Getty Images

It's often been said that khaki is a good alternative to black. Versatile, flattering and understated, it's wearable yet less severe than darker tones.

The Duchess of Sussex was evidently taken by its appeal on day one of her royal tour to Australia, New Zealand, Fiji and Tonga. She chose a dark green dress by US designer Brandon Maxwell to an afternoon reception held at Admiralty House in Sydney, hosted by the Governor General and his wife.

Getty

The look, which also comes in red, first appeared on the brand's spring/summer 2019 catwalk and is cinched in at the waist with a belt. The collar and buttoned down front add a utility appeal - a key trend for the season ahead. Although the flattering style isn't available to buy (a Google search lists only a sold-out red version at Moda Operandi), there are plenty of equally appealing khaki dresses to shop now.
