Artist Honor Prince at Coachella

February 2024 · 2 minute read

As the world continues to reel from Prince's tragic death, it would have been amiss to go about one of music's biggest festivals without honoring one of music's biggest legends. Thus, the performers at Coachella's second and final weekend kept the singer's spirit alive with a tribute-heavy lineup, whether through commemorative shout-outs or memorial covers of his biggest songs. Below, see the standout moments when Purple reigned at Coachella weekend two.

LCD Soundsystem

The band introduced their set with a video of Prince's own Coachella performance back in 2008, pictured above. (Audio recordings of the full set are now up on Soundcloud.) The footage of choice was his talked-about cover of Radiohead's "Creep." After the screening, the words "From now on, this is Prince's house" remained on the main stage, LA Times reports. LCD also performed "Controversy" as an extension of the tribute. 

Usher

The R&B star appeared as a surprise guest during Major Lazer's set for upbeat covers of Prince's "I Would Die 4 U" and "1999."

Ellie Goulding

Goulding put a dramatic spin on "When Doves Cry" by slowing it down for an emotional rendition.

Kamasi Washington

Popular saxophonist Kamasi Washington and his band played a jazzed-up version of "1999"mashed up with their original "Malcolm's Theme." 

Sufjan Stevens

During his set, the multi-talented musician teamed up with electro-soul singer Gallant for a chilling performance of the quintessential Prince anthem, "Purple Rain." The audience "hoo"-ed along.

Jack Ü

Skrillex and Diplo honored the late singer with a medley of his hits at the conclusion of their set. 

Mavis Staples

The R&B and gospel songstress paid her respects to Prince when she stopped mid-set to reminisce on her past moments with him. She then went on to sing a few lines from "Purple Rain." 

Joey Bada$$

The rapper took on his set with Prince's symbol projecting from the screens behind him. 

Guns N' Roses

Bassist Duff McKagan played the set with Prince's signature symbol emblazoned on his white bass guitar. 

Ice Cube

"I would like to dedicate this show to my man, Prince Rogers Nelson," the rapper declared during his set, with images of the singer on display behind him. 

Despacio

The DJ honored Prince by putting on a two-hour set solely featuring his songs.
