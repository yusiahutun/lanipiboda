12 Best Clothing Subscription Boxes for Women in 2024

April 2024 · 15 minute read

Want to shop for effortless, stylish looks without leaving your home? Forget scouring racks for the right size and styles — the best clothing subscription boxes for women do the shopping for you. From rental services with dedicated stylists to help you round out your capsule wardrobe or refresh your closet with the season’s latest fashions to pre-curated collections of cozy loungewear, trendy jewelry, and even swimwear, there’s a box for every body, aesthetic, and occasion. 

Whether you need to update your work look or want some cute vacation fits, we’ve compared each service’s styles, prices, and membership perks to compile this list of the 12 of the best fashion boxes for women.

Best Clothing Subscription Boxes for Women in 2023

Best Overall: Stitch Fix

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $25-$500/item
  • Free Shipping? Yes 
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Over 1,000 high-quality brands

  • Wide selection of sizes

  • Can preview stylist selections

Why It’s a Top Pick

There’s a reason Stitch Fix, one of the original online styling services, has staying power. Members have access to the latest fashions and accessories from more than 1,000 high-quality brands, including Madewell, Good American, and Kate Spade, as well as the company’s private label. Items are available in sizes XS to 3X, and the service has a large selection of petites and maternity wear. Getting your "fix" is simple: Take an online quiz to answer questions about your budget, body type, sizing, and favorite colors. A personal stylist will then send you five custom-selected pieces you can preview prior to shipping. While there is a $20 styling fee, it can be applied to any looks you decide to keep. Stylists use your picks to select items for future fixes, but you can always contact them directly to help improve your shipments with your favorite colorways or designers. Love your whole fix? You’ll get 25 percent off for keeping everything in your box. Be sure to return unwanted items within three days or request an extension to avoid getting charged for them.

Items start at $25, but some pieces can be as pricey as $500, so budget accordingly. Membership is flexible, too: Set up one-time or automatic deliveries (every two to three weeks, every month, or every two to three months). You can also buy certain plus-sized items directly from the site without a subscription.

Best for High-End Brands: Armoire

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $79/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes 
  • No. of Plans: 3

Pros & Cons 

Why It’s a Top Pick

Part personal shopping service, part clothing rental company, Armoire stocks a large selection of designer pieces from brands like Yumi Kim, Nicole Miller, and Diane Von Furstenberg. Sizes range from 0 to 24W, including maternity sizes for you to stay stylish throughout your pregnancy. It’s also a great option for special occasions: Rent something without blowing your budget on something you’ll never wear again. Unlike other best clothing subscription boxes for women, there’s no styling fee. Armoire not only has an online style quiz, but it also has one-on-one virtual styling sessions to make sure items are tailored to your individual aesthetic, fit, and lifestyle needs. 

Choose from three membership tiers that start at $79 per month ($69 per month for first-timers) for four items and top out at $249 for six items and unlimited swaps, which makes it more expensive than other styling services. But, dry cleaning and shipping are free, and for those who like to regularly refresh their wardrobe, it is still more cost-effective and eco-friendly than purchasing items individually. Memberships can be paused or canceled whenever you like, so it’s a commitment-free way to sample high-end pieces. 

Best for Trendy Items: Nuuly

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $88/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Dozens of on-trend brands

  • New clothing styles added regularly

  • Items come in a sustainable, reusable bag

Why It’s a Top Pick

Refresh your closet on the regular with Nuuly Rent, a best clothing subscription box for young women. Select six items a month from trendy designers like Farm Rio, ASTR The Label, Saylor, and Paige for a budget-friendly $88 a month, with the option to purchase at a discount or swap out when you’re done. The service is a great option for trying on-trend items without the commitment of buying — or the fast-fashion guilt. Be aware that some rental items can be more worn than others, and certain styles and sizes might not be available. Memberships can be paused or canceled anytime, with free two-day shipping and returns. 

All shipments come in a reusable bag, and you can continue your eco-conscious shopping on Nuuly Thrift, the company’s resale marketplace that has everything from vintage jewelry to gently used contemporary fashions like cashmere Everlane sweaters and Gucci platforms. Plus, you can get cash or Nuuly bucks for your purchases. The latter is worth 10 percent more when purchasing items from the shop or any URBN brand, including Anthropologie, Free People, and Urban Outfitters.

Best for Athletic Clothing: Ellie

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $49.95/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Why It’s a Top Pick 

On a budget but tired of wearing the same old black leggings to the gym? Ellie’s monthly subscription boxes feature high-quality activewear in vibrant colors, breathable fabrics, and flattering styles. Choose from preselected bundles each month — think cozy jogger sets, matching high-impact bras, bottoms, and slimming leggings — plus two essential workout props like resistance bands and jump ropes. 

Starting at $49.95 per month with no added styling fee, the subscriptions are super affordable and can be paused or canceled anytime. The 30-day return window is generous compared to similar clothing boxes for women, and you can also purchase items you like for a discount. Note that sizing extends only to XXL.

Best for Personal Styling: UrbaneBox

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $74.99/box
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Extensive style quiz

  • Free exchanges

  • Great customer service

Why It’s a Top Pick 

While UrbaneBox was initially a men’s-only subscription box, it now offers the same styling services for women. To get started, take a style quiz to choose your sizes, favorite colorways, and overall style vibe, and you’ll receive two to three items per month selected for you by a personal stylist. Heavy on structured separates like blazers and tailored pants, the boxes are great for updating your work wardrobe if you are between sizes XS and XL. 

At $75 a month, the service is more expensive than its competitors, and styles are not as varied as you’d get with other companies. Customer service, however, is exceptional; you can even email your personal stylist to give feedback on each month’s picks. And you’re shopping for a good cause: UrbaneBox has donated clothing to City Missions and children programs.

Best for Swimwear: Beachly

Key Specs

  • Average Price: Pay annually for $90/box
  • Free Shipping? No
  • No. of Plans: 2

Pros & Cons 

Pros

  • Access to a member market with premium brands and discounts

  • Pieces tailored to location

  • Boxes include beauty products, accessories, and novelty items

Why It’s a Top Pick 

Whether your vibe is coastal grandma or Amalfi coast, Beachly is the go-to subscription box for all things surf and sun. In addition to a large collection of swimwear from L Space and Amuse Society, boxes include all the essential items for a beach getaway: think flowy dresses, knit cardigans, tote bags, plus accessories and beauty products like lip balm, fragrance, and hydrating facial mists.

Boxes are preselected seasonally, so you can’t choose your items. The Winter 2022 box included a quilted jacket, sweater, and beach blanket. Your subscription gets you access to an online members-only market stocked with exclusive brands as well as special discounts. ​​Annual memberships are $90 per box, and seasonal memberships are $99 per box, so they are on the pricier side.

Best for Jewelry: Rocksbox

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $21/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Large assortment of contemporary, demi-fine jewelry

  • Can preview stylist picks

  • Affordable

Why It’s a Top Pick

Do your accessories need a boost? Whether you’re into glam statement necklaces or everyday hoop earrings, subscription service Rocksbox offers a large selection of the latest jewelry and trinkets like rings, hair claws, and more. For an affordable $21 monthly fee, you’ll get three pieces a month from brands like Gorjana, Kate Spade, Kendra Scott, and Sophie Harper. 

Signing up is easy: Take a style quiz to set your style, metal, and stone preferences, and then a stylist will generate a wish list that you can add to each month’s subscription. You can preview pieces before they ship, so there are no surprises. And if you love something, you can purchase it for a discounted amount. If not, return your items in the prepaid return shipping label and swap them out monthly to keep your accessories fresh. Note: Rocksbox’s jewelry collection does not include fine stones or diamonds, and the service might not be the best fit for those with metal allergies. 

Best Variety: Wantable

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $20-$150/item
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 4

Pros & Cons

Why It’s a Top Pick 

Get the latest fashions without commitment to buying with women’s clothing delivery service Wantable. The company carries items from top brands like BB Dakota, Levi's, and Paige in sizes XS to 3X. You can choose from several box types, including athleisure, loungewear, and seasonal styles like “light layers” and “vacation style.” 

Share your favorite looks, budget, and color and style preferences through an online quiz, and then a personal stylist will choose seven pieces that will be delivered to your doorstep. The $20 styling fee will be credited to items you keep, and you receive a 20 percent discount if you keep five or more looks. Be sure to return unwanted pieces within the five-day window. Because Wantable does not require a subscription, you can refresh your wardrobe as frequently — or infrequently — as you’d like. Note that shipments can take up to 14 days to arrive, so it’s not a great option for last-minute special events or occasions.

Best for Workwear: Le Tote

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $59/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes 
  • No. of Plans: 2

Pros & Cons 

Cons

  • No plus, tall, or petite sizes

  • Limited availability for popular items

  • No stylist assistance

Why It’s a Top Pick 

Le Tote offers flexible membership plans that allow you to choose the exact number of clothing items and accessories, plus a large assortment of maternity styles, to round out your professional wardrobe. Fill out a style questionnaire, then select the number of items you’d like in each tote. Shop “The New 9 to 5” collection, stocked with bold blazers, midi dresses, and plaid trousers from mid-range, work-appropriate designers like Fore, French Connection, and Vince Camuto.

One plan offers three items ands two accessories; the other comes with four items. You can swap anything that doesn’t work within seven days and keep your favorites at 20 to 70 percent off the retail price. While Le Tote has a large selection of maternity clothing, its sizing only extends to 16W with no plus, petite, or tall options, so it’s not a great option for those who fall outside of that range. You’ll also be on your own to select which styles and sizes work best, and sometimes, more popular items get nabbed quickly, so plan on having backup picks.

Best for Intimates and Loungewear: Adore Me

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $39.99/item
  • Free Shipping: Yes
  • No. of Plans: 2

Pros & Cons 

Why It’s a Top Pick 

From statement bustiers and elegant lingerie sets to bold swimsuits and cozy pajamas, Adore Me is one the best fashion boxes for intimates and loungewear. Band sizing ranges from 30 to 46 and A to I cups, with apparel ranging from XS to 4X. Items in each shipment cost $19.99 to $59.95, making it both inclusive and affordable.

Take an online fashion quiz to choose the types of pieces you’d like to receive as well as your size and style preferences, which range from “romantic” and “bold and sexy” to “daytime chic.” (Some customers report it can take a few boxes to really nail down your favorite style.) The first Elite Box comes with a $10 styling fee, and each subsequent box has a $20 styling fee; these fees can be credited toward the first item you keep. You’ll receive a curated box of four to seven looks, with seven days to try on before returning. The company has two membership tiers, including a VIP program that gives you access to new collection drops, exclusive sales, $10 off each set, and other perks. Regardless of membership tier, you can pause or cancel shipments anytime.

Best for Plus Sizes: Gwynnie Bee

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $49/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 6

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Large selection of trendy brands

  • Online marketplace for buying and selling gently used items

  • 50 percent discount for first month of rentals

Why It’s a Top Pick 

Gwynnie Bee offers a large selection of separates, denim, and outerwear from brands like Kut from the Kloth, Sanctuary Clothing, and Vince Camuto in women’s sizes 0-32. Unlike other clothing boxes for women, there’s no stylist assistance, but the plus-size options and range of designers and styles can’t be beat. Browse the expansive online collection and add your favorite pieces to your online “closet.” Then, choose your plan, which starts at $49/month for one piece and goes up to $199 for 10 pieces — a retail value of nearly $4,000, but on the pricier side for rental subscriptions. The service will send you available looks from your closet, prioritizing your top picks.

You can keep items for as long as you want, then swap out for fresh styles when you’re ready, without paying for dry cleaning or shipping. Or keep them forever — the service offers a discount for all rental item purchases. Shop for even better deals on Gwynnie Bee’s members-only marketplace for gently used clothing and accessories.

Best for Basics: Basic Woman

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $19.99/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes (U.S. only)
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Why It’s a Top Pick 

Socks and delicates aren’t the most glamorous wardrobe staples, but even they need a refresh now and again. Enter Basic Woman, a women’s subscription box that takes care of all of the basics for you. With items in sizes XS to 3XL, each subscription includes a set of underwear, socks, and a top in soft, sustainable fabrics like bamboo. The camisoles, crop tops, and tanks are even breathable and fashionable enough to double as workout wear, but overall, the service lacks the variety of other subscription boxes. 

Packaging is as eco-friendly as the clothing, and at $24.99 a month for the monthly plan, Basic Woman is a budget-friendly way to update your closet with those little necessities. The subscription auto-renews, so if it’s too frequent for your needs, you can pause or cancel as needed, especially if you find you don’t need monthly shipments.

Final Verdict

When it comes to clothing boxes for women, Stitch Fix comes out on top. The service includes personal styling and a wide range of sizes and access to the latest looks from more than 1,000 top designers — all at an affordable price. 

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do You Pick the Clothes You Get in a Clothing Subscription Box?

    Whether you need the perfect dress for a fancy night out, beachwear for that upcoming tropical vacation, or a few pieces to elevate your everyday look, the best subscription boxes for women are full of fashionable finds that get delivered straight to your door. With some services, like Wantable and Armoire, you not only get access to a dedicated stylist to help you choose unique looks tailored to your body and personal aesthetic but you can preview items before they are delivered. Others, like Beachly and Ellie, are pre-curated, so you can’t swap out items if they aren’t a good fit. Be sure to review each company’s collection and subscription details before committing to a service.

  • Can You Return Clothes That Come in Clothing Subscription Boxes?

    There are two types of subscription boxes: rentals and curated boxes. Rental subscriptions are like Nuuly and Gwynnie Bee, where you pay a monthly subscription fee and keep items until you’re ready to swap them out for something new. Curated boxes, on the other hand, like Basic Woman and Beachly feature preselected pieces. With rentals, you’re able to return and swap items for new sizes or styles within a certain time frame. But with curated boxes, return policies are not as flexible. Depending on the company, you can often exchange damaged items or return unworn items for a refund, but you’re stuck if something in your shipment just isn’t your style. Make sure to check each company’s website for specific policies prior to placing your order. 

  • What Brands Are Included in Clothing Subscription Boxes?

    One of the best parts of a clothing subscription box is access to dozens of name brands for a fraction of retail cost. You can expect to find items from top clothing designers like Yumi Kim, Nicole Miller, Paige, and Vince Camuto, plus accessories from Kate Spade, Kendra Scott, and Sophie Harper. Other boxes, like Ellie and Basic Woman, carry their own private labels, so members get exclusive access to those pieces.

  • What Sizes Can You Get in a Clothing Subscription Box?

    Sizing varies from box to box, but all of the ones included on our list stock at least sizes XS to XL. For plus sizes, Gwynnie Bee carries a great selection of on-trend clothing in sizes 0 to 32. Stitch Fix and Armoire have a large selection of maternity clothing to keep you stylish throughout your pregnancy, and Stitch Fix offers maternity pieces as well as a large selection of petites.

Methodology 

To find the best clothing subscription boxes for women, we reviewed more than 20 clothing subscription services, rating each company on the following criteria:

  • Size range
  • Subscription price
  • Number of items per box
  • Shipping fees
  • Available discounts
  • Brand variety
  • Rental period

Subscriptions that offer a wide array of clothing options at relatively affordable prices did particularly well in the scoring phase, as did services with low or free shipping costs, membership discounts, and flexible try-on periods. Companies with limited delivery capabilities didn’t make it past our initial research stage.
