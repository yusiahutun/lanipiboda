Meghan Markle's Pippa Small jewellery has a sweet connection to Prince Charles

June 2024 · 2 minute read

The Duchess of Sussex mastered maternity chic in a lack, floor-length gown by Givenchy for her surprise appearance at the British Fashion Awards on Monday night. She also broke royal rules with a statement manicure. But it was her choice of jewellery that carried a special meaning - and honoured her father-in-law, Prince Charles.

Joe Maher/BFC//Getty Images

Meghan accessorised her look with a trio of gold bangles and a pair of stud earrings from British jeweller, Pippa Small. The pieces were from Small's Turquoise Mountain Foundation, an Afghanistan-based charity that was founded by Prince Charles in 2006. The organisation supports and trains local artisans and businesses while working to preserve their unique culture, heritage and traditions.

"Honoured to see the beautiful Duchess of Sussex wearing our bangles and stud earrings proudly made by artisans of the @TurquoiseMountain foundation in Afghanistan," Small shared on Instagram. "Working to train and employ young men and women in conflict areas."

Meghan chose the Nosheen Stud earrings (£50), which were handcrafted by artisans in Kabul, paired with the gold plate Oshna (£115), Omeen (£160) and Wajiha (£360) bangles. It's not the first time she was worn the collection, either. The Duchess wore the same bracelets during the royal tour of Fiji back in October.

Earlier in the month, following Princess Eugenie's wedding, Small tweeted to say she was "proud" to see Meghan wearing diamond rings, a bracelet and earrings from the brand.

Perhaps Meghan's thoughtful choice of jewellery will inspire some Christmas shopping?
