The leader of the Church of Scientology, David Miscavige, went on a series of foul-mouth rants at employees while he desperately tried to sabotage a journalist filming a documentary about the organization, according to new court documents.

Miscavige, who is Tom Cruise’s closest friend and was the best man at the actor’s wedding to Katie Holmes, berated the organization’s spokesman Tommy Davis, the son of Hollywood actress Ann Archer, and Mike Rinder, a former senior executive, in vile text messages during a 2007 church operation to thwart the journalist’s investigations.

He called the staffers names including, ‘generality infested c***-sucking motherf*****’ and ‘counter-intentioned c***sucker’.

Mike Rinder, a former executive at the church, wrote in a court declaration filed on January 1, that he had been asked to 'discourage John Sweeney from completing a documentary on Mr. Miscavige and Scientology'

Davis and Rinder had been tracking BBC host John Sweeney who had come to the United States to film a documentary about Scientology for the program Panorama.

Rinder, who later defected from the church, wrote in a court declaration filed on January 1, that he had been asked to ‘discourage John Sweeney from completing a documentary on Mr. Miscavige and Scientology’, according to RadarOnline.com.

'We reported the movements of Sweeney and team to Miscavige almost daily and sometimes several times per day,’ he said in the court document.

In one response to Miscavige, Rinder told his boss that Sweeney had been ‘repeatedly exposed as a bigot and tabloid bottom feeder’, according to RadarOnline.

Not content, as Davis and Rinder continued to track Sweeney’s movements, Miscavige berated the pair for their incompetence.

Rinder’s declaration comes as part of a Texas lawsuit brought against the Church of Scientology by Monique Rathbun, the wife of a one-time church executive, who has alleged Miscavige and the church stalked and harassed her and her husband over the last four years.
