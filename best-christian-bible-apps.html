The 6 Best Christian Bible Apps for Android and iPhone

May 2024 Â· 7 minute read

Many Christians prefer the good old print Bibles, but there are mobile-based Bible apps that you can use in situations where you don't have access to your print Bible. Several people already use these apps in all situations, but picking one that is good for you can be challenging.

So, below, we've listed some of the best Christian Bible apps available for iPhone and Android devices. Regardless of what you're looking for, you should find one that suits you among these.

1. YouVersion Bible

Close

YouVersion Bible is arguably the best Bible app on this list and comes with many features. Right on the homepage, you'll see the verse of the day alongside the verse of the day story and a guided prayer based on the day's verse.

You can share the verse of the day with friends through email, WhatsApp, and other social media apps. You can also create a community of friends on the app, where you can discuss the Bible with them.

The app has nearly 70 Bible versions, and you can download any number of them to read offline. Depending on your location, the app also supports several local languages in which you can read, in addition to global languages like English, French, and German.

When busy, you can listen to the word being read with the audio version, which is only a click away from the written version. With the Plans feature, you can also plan your Bible reading to fit your schedule.

There is also a prayer for each day, and you can keep a record of your Bible study with notes and badges. Like most other Bible apps, you can use the YouVersion Bible app completely for free.

Download: YouVersion Bible for Android | iOS (Free)

2. The Olive Tree Bible App

Close

Another Bible app that is excellent for studying is Olive Tree. Like YouVersion, it comes with a verse for each day and has various reading plans you can adopt. This could be sections of the Bible in a few months or the whole Bible in a year.

You can access the app without registering, but there are features you can't access until you do so. The app is majorly a Bible study app, so signing up and subscribing allows you to do guided Bible study with the help of Bible scholars.

And like YouVersion, you can also track your Bible reading and take notes as you go, bookmark favorite chapters, and even highlight whatever you find worth saving for later. You can also save passages and categorize your library with tags for easier access. Olive Tree also has a long list of Bible versions, so you can read and compare scriptures from different versions.

You get to learn about specific subject matters such as faith, forgiveness, love, etc., with specific Bible reading plans, all packed into this app.

Download: Olive Tree Bible App for Android | iOS (Free)

3. Holy Bible Multi Versions

Close

This is another multi-version Bible app with a straightforward and plain user interface. In addition, it is an offline app, so you can study the Bible any time you want, even when your internet connection isn't working. By default, only the Good News Bible translation will be downloaded offline when you install the app. However, you can download other versions available inside the app by yourself.

If you forget a verse reference, you can simply search for the wordings or a keyword, which will either bring up the specific verse or verses with the keyword. Holy Bible Multi Version also separates the Old and New Testaments for better organization.

Additionally, it has a Verse by Category section, which lists different themes, and when you select, display specific bible verses that talk about the theme. You'll also find a daily verse like other bible apps that you can share with friends and family whenever you like.

It also doesn't miss out on basic features like bookmarks, note-taking, favorites, copy, and share functionality. Unfortunately, although this is one of the best free bible apps for Android, it isn't available for iOS, so iPhone users have to resort to one of the other options.

Download: Holy Bible Multi Version for Android (Free)

4. Bible Gateway

Close

Bible Gateway is a Bible study resource with features such as devotionals, reading plans, commentaries, and study Bibles. You can search the entire Bible for a keyword or a verse and choose from different Bible versions.

The verse of the day is the first thing you see every day, and you can take notes on your own or when listening to a sermon. The app's scripture engagement exercises also help you interact with the Bible more practically. Like other bible apps in this list, you can favorite or highlight interesting verses and even share them.

All these features come with the free version, but you can upgrade to the premium version to unlock even more exciting features. You can start a 30-day free trial before subscribing to the full version. Chapters are titled to give you a clear idea of what you're reading.

Download: Bible Gateway for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

5. NASB Bible

Close

The name New American Standard Bible (NASB) sounds like a Bible for only Americans, but anyone can actually enjoy reading this Bible. It has a simplistic interface with minimal color or other distractions. The app, though a single Bible version, has all the features the other apps possess and even more.

The daily verse section comes with one random verse, a verse from the Gospels, and a verse from the Psalms. This makes it even richer than the other apps. It has reading plans through the Bible in one year, 180 days, and 90 days, in canonical, chronological, and historical order.

You can sync the app between devices by signing in using your Google account to access all the features on several Android devices at once. The app also lets you modify the user interface to your taste by changing the color combinations, font family, font size, and line spacing. However, if you're annoyed by in-app ads, you can always upgrade to the premium version.

Download: NASB Bible for Android (Free, subscription available)

6. Tecarta Bible App

Close

Looking for a simple yet comprehensive best Bible app to use for your study? Tecarta Bible App may be what you're looking for. It has several features yet is very easy to use thanks to its simple user interface. The app gives you access to 40 translations, over 13 commentaries, 90 study Bibles, 12 devotional Bibles, more than 18 devotionals, and several audio Bibles.

There are also Bible versions in different languages, including Spanish. And if you don't know Spanish, you can quickly learn it with one of the best Spanish learning apps.

The app's homepage greets you with a series of verses every day. While studying, you can easily take notes for later references. You can also sync purchased content across devices with limitless resources at your disposal. And if needed, you can switch between dark and light modes for the display to suit your eyesight needs.

Like many other Bible apps, you can use the audio assistant to read through when reading is impractical.

Download: Tecarta Bible App for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

Bible Study Made Easy With These Apps

Almost all the Bible apps we listed here have unique features, but all of them aim to serve one purpose: make Bible study as easy and fun as possible. So, it doesn't really matter which app you choose to install in the end.

Even the premium ones have free resources that will more than meet your study needs, and any of them will likely satisfy you.
