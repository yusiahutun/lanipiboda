Beach bliss: The best stretches of sand in Greece and Cyprus

June 2024 · 3 minute read

The best hidden, family-friendly and picturesque stretches of sand on the Greek islands and Cyprus

Think of Greece, and you’ll probably conjure up an image of lush, rocky coves dropping down to a stretch of soft sand and the mottled blues of the Mediterranean. 

Some of Europe’s best beaches are located on Greece’s islands and the neighbouring nation of Cyprus. Whether you’re after an unspoilt, empty stretch of white, pink or even black sand, or an activity-packed beach where the fun continues after sunset, here’s a guide to the best, from specialist online travel agent On the Beach.

Perissa Beach, Santorini

Located on romantic Santorini, the volcanic black sands of Perissa Beach are in stark contrast to Santorini’s traditional white architecture. This stunning beach has plenty of family-friendly facilities and great places to eat.

Miti Beach, Halkidiki

Jutting out into the blue-green waters, this long corridor of sand is renowned for its beauty. A flawless tongue of fine white sand, it stretches out to sea and is shaped throughout the day by the tides.

Flawless: The sandy peninsula of Miti Beach on Halkidiki is beach paradise

Elafonissi Beach, Crete

What is in fact a sandbar connecting Elafonissi island to the mainland, through warm lagoon water, this beach is famed for its ombré sand that blends from white to coral pink.

CYPRIOT SANDS

Cyprus boasts some world-class beaches, and they’re an important reason the island is such a popular holiday destination for all ages. Whether you’re a Cyprus regular or you’ve never visited before, this sunny island has a swathe of sand perfect for you!

Fig Tree Bay, Protaras

Fig Tree Bay, above, gets its name from the pretty backdrop of fig trees that line the beach. This, paired with clear, shallow waters and colourful fish, give you a family-friendly snorkelling spot to fall in love with.

Lady’s Mile, Limassol

The longest beach in Cyprus, Lady’s Mile offers golden sand and great views of the surrounding area, as well as natural shade if you need to cool off. It is also a popular spot for windsurfing.

Konnos Bay, Protaras

Konnos Bay in Protaras is a great choice for a relaxed day beside the seaside. The water here is crystal clear and very calm, making it perfect for families with small children.

The family friendly Konnos Bay in Protaras, Cyprus, boasts calm waters and is great for kids

Shipwrecked shores

Often known as Shipwreck Bay, the only way to reach Navagio Beach in Zante is by boat.

Dwarfed on left and right by white cliffs, this crescent of fine limestone gravel has become famous for the imposing, rust-covered skeleton of the run-aground vessel that towers in the middle of the beach.

The freightliner, the MV Panagiotis – rumoured to be crewed by smugglers at the time – was washed ashore in stormy weather in 1980, and has lain abandoned on the beach ever since.

Navagio Beach on the Greek island of Zante is a cobalt blue cove with a 40-year-old shipwreck

Set sail to Navagio Beach from Zakynthos Town, or a nearby port, and you’ll be met with the sight of the ship as you round the corner into the cobalt blue cove.

You might also see several other boats, as the beach has become a popular tourist spot. Go earlier in the morning or at sunset to avoid too many other people.
