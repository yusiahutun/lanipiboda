1. Shania Twain

  • Date of birth: August 28, 1965
  • Age: 58 years old
  • Years active: 1993 – present
  • Top songs: Man, I Feel Like A Woman, You’re Still The One, Whose Bed Have Your Boots Been Under, and That Don’t Impress Me Much

Without a doubt, Shania Twain was the queen of 90s country music. The Canadian singer-songwriter has sold over 100 million records, making her one of the best-selling music artists ever.

Her success in the 90s gained her the title of “Queen of Pop. She released big hits that people enjoy today, like Man, I Feel Like A Woman, You’re Still The One, Whose Bed Have Your Boots Been Under, and That Don’t Impress Me Much.

The renowned artist is a five-time Grammy Award winner, and her third album, Come Over, became the best-selling album by a female country music singer.

2. Faith Hill

Faith Hill is another immensely successful female country music singer, selling over 50 million albums worldwide. Her first two albums, released in the 90s, were enormous successes that placed her three songs on Billboard's country music charts.

Although Faith today is known because of her marriage to Tim McGraw, she was already successful in the music industry before the relationship even started. Faith Hill is also an upcoming actress and appears on the popular hit show Yellowstone.

And this was not the start; she had already gone mainstream in 1991 when she released She’s in Love with the Boy. The songstress had a collection of hit songs that were popular in the 90s.

5. Martina McBride

  • Date of birth: July 29, 1966
  • Age: 57 years old
  • Years active: 1988– present
  • Top songs: The Time Has Come, Evolution album

Martina is remarkable in the country music scene, renowned for her soprano singing range and country pop material. The songstress has fourteen studio albums, two greatest hits compilations, one live album, and two additional compilation albums.

Martina gained fame for stating, I’m Little, But I am Loud. She released her debut single in 1992, titled The Time Has Come, which reached number 23 on the United States charts.

Her style differed slightly from the rest as she blended country, pop, and contemporary Christian music. This unique blend helped her stand out among old female country singers of the 1990s.

Did you know that Joe Dee Messina has had six number-one singles on the Billboard country music charts? She has also been honoured by the Country Music Association and the Academy of Country Music and nominated for two Grammy Awards. Indeed, she is a master of her craft.

She entered the music scene in 1997 with a self-titled album with popular songs like Heads Carolina, Tails California, and You’re Not In Kansas Anymore. Jo Dee Messina’s second album, I’m Alright, brought her immense success with the title track Bye, Bye and Stand Beside Me.

These popular songs made Messina the first female artist to have three multi-week number-one songs on the same album.

Are there black female country singers of the 1990s?

Various black female country music singers in the 90s were prominent. These musicians included Chante Moore, Monie Love and Rissi Palmer. Chante Moore’s hit Chante’s Got a Man reached number 10 on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1999.

Who is the queen of 90s country?
