Post Malone ethnicity and early life

Post Malone was born on July 4, 1995, in Syracuse, New York. Most people ask, "Who is Post Malone's father? And where is Post Malone originally from?" Post Malone's mom and dad are Rich Post and Nicole Frazier, residents of Walton, New York. Post Malone ethnic background can be described as white American with Italian roots.

His dad and stepmother Jodie raised him. He settled on the nickname Post Malone while aged about 15 years. Initially, most people thought this stage name emulated Karl Malone's, the NBA player. He, however, explained that he relied on a "rap name generator" to create the name "Malone".

What religion is Post Malone practising? The rap artist has not come out publicly about his religious affiliation. For his high school education, he enrolled at the Grapevine High School. His dad was already into music, working as a disk jockey in his earlier years. He is the one who introduced Malone to a range of genres. He relentlessly learned to play the strings. His desire for learning the instrument was stirred by Guitar Hero, a video game that was quite popular by then. Professional career journey

Post Malone relocated to Los Angeles in unison with Jason Probst, a friend he had known for quite some time. It was after moving to LA that the artist delved into music as his full-time livelihood. He joined forces with Probst, alongside other musicians and producers, to come up with BLCKVRD, a music group. He lived in San Fernando Valley.

During his stay here, he got to meet other talents like Rich and Down, both from FKI. He also worked with Rx Kudo, who was the producer for hits such as White Iverson. Interestingly, the track was recorded just two days after composing. The song, in part, was written in reference to NBA's Allen Iverson, uploading it on SoundCloud in 2015.

Six months down the line (on July 19, that year), he released the official video of the song. The track became a massive hit, receiving praise from stars such as Wiz Khalifa and Mac Miller. Just one month after the release, it hit a million views, earning him acknowledgement by various recording labels.

Accolades and major works

Post has had a successful career and has gotten his fair share of achievements. He did the song Rock Star alongside 21 Savage, a hit that held the top position on the Billboard Hot 100, a spot that was held consecutively for eight weeks.

It was after this that Rolling Stone magazine named him among the country's most popular artists. Furthermore, the track won Billboard's Top Rap Song award. Post also released his track Psycho in February 2018, which debuted at the second position on Billboard Hot 100. This was his third entry among the top ten.

His project titled Beerbongs & Bentleys was similarly successful, breaking Spotify's first-day streaming records. It hit approximately 78.7 million streams across the globe, debuting in the first position on the Billboard 200 list. During the first seven days, it had moved over 461,000 album pieces, 153,000 of this originating from pure sales.
