3 ways to mix and match your separates

February 2024 · 3 minute read

We all have those days: you’re rifling through your wardrobe, nothing seems to work and you lose sight of what actually goes together.

But don't panic, separates shouldn't be stressful. With the new season in full swing, now is the time to invest in simple pieces that'll last a lifetime, allowing you to mix and match till your heart’s content.

Not sure where to start? From flattering blazers to killer LBDs, The Outnet is full of designer classics at up to 75% off so you can find the perfect pairings without blowing the budget.

So grab your credit card and take note of these power combinations.

Tailored

Street style is a constant testament to the high impact of monochromatic dressing, making for a look that’s instantly sleek and sophisticated.

Christian Vierig//Getty Images

One of the simplest ways to master the trend is with tailored separates in neutral colours. Take this sharp Lanvin jacket — it’s an investment piece that works beautifully with a va-va-voom midi skirt in the office or a pair of relaxed jeans for the weekend. And the best part is it won't date.

Ruche the sleeves for added nonchalance and add flair with a statement necklace and on-point heels.

Tonal

Once you've mastered the art of matching sets, why not take it to the next level?

Christian Vierig//Getty Images

Fight the autumnal blues by embracing one vivid shade top-to-toe, whether bold or pastel. It's the chicest way to wear colour this season.

Don’t be afraid to mix and match shades of the same hue. This double-breasted blazer and cropped trousers combo makes for a sartorially confident combination.

Counterbalance a sea of colour by slipping in a grey cashmere sweater or turning to your trusty camel pieces. Maybe, just maybe, you'll look as sharp as Gigi Hadid, tonal dressing supreme.

The Outnet

SHOP: W118 by Walter Baker blazer, £190; Joseph trousers, £213; Valentino pumps, £396; Ben Amun earrings, £51

Texture

One joy of separates is you can explore less obvious combinations. Clashing prints can be tricky to master so ditch them in favour of experimenting with different textures instead.

Christian Vierig

A mix of materials adds an interesting twist to your outfit and prevents it from looking one-dimensional. Try layering hard-edged tailored pieces over a soft, silky, billowing skirt. We're calling dibs on this sumptuous design from Roland Mouret.

Punctuate with ladylike accessories and a smattering of gold jewellery to pull the outfit together. Et voilà: you have a classy, cool autumn look.
