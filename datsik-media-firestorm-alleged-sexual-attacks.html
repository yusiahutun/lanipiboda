Datsik In Media Firestorm Over Alleged Sexual Attacks

March 2024 · 3 minute read

LOS ANGELES (CelebrityAccess) Datsik has been accused multiple times of sexual assault over the past 24 hours, with Space Jesus removing himself from Datsik’s current “Ninja Nation” tour.

Last night’s show by the dubstep star in Des Moines, Iowa, was canceled without explanation but after a day of accusations trended on Twitter.

“Datsik f—ed my 18 year old friend after getting her so drunk she could barely stand last year,” one Twitter user claimed, according to the Syracuse Post-Standard (Datsik, aka Troy Beetles, played in the city recently).

Multiple female fans said they received backstage passes with the word “Tulsa” written on them.

“Tulsa is ‘A Slut’ backwards and they gave them to girls I know at the Syracuse NY stop at the [Westcott Theater] as well,” a Facebook user wrote. “That’s nothing new, but have been seeing many status (sic) about [Datsik] today and it doesn’t look good.”

Website EDMsauce.com said some brands have already began to dissacoiate with the DJ.

Datsik posted the following statement:

“Yo everyone. This is a very serious matter to me… There have been recent allegations against me for things I have not done. I am a vibe reader, I hang out with a ton of people after every show, and always keep a positive vibe. I would never bend anyone against their will and I mean that,” he wrote.

“I’m an adult, I’ve seen and been thru it all. But these claims are shaking me to the core. This type of s— is detrimental to my persona as I truly take pride in being myself and being honest. I want everyone to know that I am being the best Datsik I can… It really breaks my heart when people feel upset at the end of the day because I am a good person and would never take advantage of anyone,” he continued. “This is my life and my passion as I know it. I care so much about my fans and my music and what you all think of me. I will make a bigger effort to make sure that nothing I do will be misinterpreted in the future.”

He added that “Tulsa” was written by “a f**king tool on tour” who was subsequently fired.
