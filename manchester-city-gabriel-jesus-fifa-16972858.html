Manchester City wonderkid Gabriel Jesus is FIFA 17s new Neymar in latest update

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Pep Guardiola might be going through a tough patch right now and with Spurs playing the Sky Blues this weekend things are only set to get tougher.

However, there is some good news, as Manchester City have at last announced the signing of Brazilian wonderkid Gabriel Jesus.

Even better news is that the team's latest signing is now available to play as in FIFA 17 on PS4, Xbox One and PC, thanks to a recent EA Sports update to the game.

City originally agreed to the £27m deal for Jesus in August but the player remained at Palmeiras for the remainder of the Brazilian league season, helping them claim the title in November with a game to spare - and now he has finally available in the game.

In addition, the 19-year-old forward is also one of the game's best talents with a potential rating of 90.

To put that into perspective the only U-20's with 90 potential in the entire game are Marcus Rashford (Manchester United), Ousmane Dembele (Borussia Dortmund), Anthony Martial (Manchester United) and Dele Alli (Tottenham Hotspur).

Keep reading to take a look at some stats for the Citizen's exciting young forward.

FIFA 17 - Manchester City Wonderkid Gabriel Jesus View gallery

Gabriel Jesus' overall rating is a decent 78, which is probably boosted by his occasional appearances for the Brazilian national team and the numerous sporting journalists who've labelled the forward one of the best young prospects from Brazil since Neymar.

As a comparison, Jesus rating puts him slightly higher than fellow City striker Kelechi Iheanacho (76) and also just ahead of Marcus Rashford (77), a stat that's sure to please any City fan looking for bragging rights when talking to any Manchester United fan

It's his other stats though that really stand out, making Jesus one of the best players in the game.

He's super fast with 88 acceleration, 85 sprint speed and incredibly agile with a whopping 92 agility rating.

In addition, he's not a pushover with 82 reactions, 91 balance and dribbling stats (85 dribbling, 83 ball control) that could give some of the games best players a run for their money.

And even at 19, he's already got himself an impressive 80 rating for finishing, making him pretty deadly in front of goal too.

In other FIFA 17 related news, EA Sports yesterday released new FIFA 17 1.05 update on PS4 and Xbox One.

Among several cosmetic details, the update also made some massive changes to the games FUT Ultimate Team mode.
