Erin Andrews' stalker explains how he got room next to ESPN presenter

September 2024 · 6 minute read

The stalker who posted naked footage of ESPN presenter Erin Andrews online has explained how he managed to make the film.

Michael David Barrett, who was jailed for 20 months after admitting making the film, said he got Andrews' room number after calling from the hotel lobby, before requesting to stay next door to her.

Once in the room, he described modifying the peephole in her door with a hacksaw before listening for her to get out of the shower, then filming her with his cell phone.

Barrett revealed his 'ill-conceived' plan during videotaped testimony Monday at a civil trial in which Andrews is suing Barrett and the Nashville Marriott at Vanderbilt University for $75million, saying they missed opportunities to protect her. 

Michael David Barrett said he listened for Erin Andrews (pictured in court Tuesday) to get into the shower. Once the water stopped he went back to the peephole and filmed her naked with his cell phone

Barrett (pictured in 2009), a former insurance executive from Chicago, has revealed how he managed to film then-ESPN reporter Erin Andrews naked in her hotel room

During video testimony from 2012, Barrett said he called an operator from a hotel house phone and asked for Andrews' room. The room number appeared on the phone display, allowing him to book the one next door

Barrett revealed that he devised his plan in September 2008 after going to Nashville for a business trip, before finding out that Andrews was staying in a hotel nearby.

He said he went to check in but was told a room he had requested was not available, and so went to wait in the hotel restaurant, according to Page Six.

There, he used a house phone and called the operator, asking to be put through to Andrews' room, which the operator did.

He said: 'On the house phone it shows a room number, so I knew what room she was in.'

Barrett then confessed to going upstairs and seeing the maid cleaning the room next to Andrews, so he knew the occupant must be checking out.

Once Barrett had heard Andrews leave her room he pulled the peephole out of her door and modified it with a hacksaw allowing him to look inside (recreation pictured)

He added: 'I went back to the front desk and told them, “That room is open. Can I check into that room?” They said, “Let’s check and see,” and went back. 

'After a few minutes, they came back out, and they checked me into that room.

'I went to my room. As I was coming to the room, I could actually hear her talking on the phone, so I knew it was the right room.'

HOW TO KNOW IF YOUR PEEPHOLE HAS BEEN HACKED 

Peepholes use a series of convex and concave lenses to manipulate light in such a way that people inside a room can see out, while people outside cannot see in.

However, by removing one or more of these lenses, peepholes can be modified so they are little more than a hole in the door.

Looking through the peephole from inside, guests should have a clear view of what is on the other side.

If the view is cloudy or obscured, it could be a sign the device has been tampered with.

If you find this is the case in your room, request to be transferred to another, Michael Forrest Jones, CEO of Beechmont Hotels, said.

An alternative would be to block the peephole from inside with a wad of twisted paper, and only remove it when needed to answer the door.

Barrett said he waited in his hotel room 'dealing with a few things' before hearing Andrews leaving her room, at which point he went into the corridor outside.

There, he pulled the peephole out of her door and cut off part of it with a hacksaw, allowing him to see in from outside before replacing it.

When Andrews arrived back to her room, Barrett said he listened through the wall for the sound of a shower running.

After the shower stopped, Barrett said he walked out into the corridor and began filming Andrews through the hacked peephole, knowing she would be naked in the room.

Asked why he filmed Andrews, Barrett said he was in debt at the time, having made a series of bad investments shortly before the financial crash.

He hoped to raise cash by selling the footage, but when he was unable to do so, he posted it online for free.

Questioned over why he did so, he responded: 'That is a great question, and I really have thought about it over the years, and I don’t have a good answer for it. I don’t know.'

Barrett said the only reason he picked Andrews was because she was popular and he saw that she was trending on Yahoo. 

He posted the recordings online after celebrity gossip website TMZ refused to buy the footage.

Barrett spent more than two and a half years in federal prison after he admitted to renting hotel rooms next to the Andrews three times and shooting videos of her in Nashville and Columbus, Ohio. 

He was an executive with a Chicago-area insurance company when he shot the footage of Andrews in the Nashville hotel in September of 2008.

Andrews is suing Barrett and the Marriott hotel for $75million. Erin's mother Pamela Andrews (pictured on Monday in court) testified that the incident has left her daughter a 'shell' of her former self

Andrews said the Marriott hotel at Vanderbilt University in Tennessee missed opportunities to protect her from Barrett, who managed to check into three rooms next door to her on three separate occasions

Barrett, who traveled extensively in his former job, admitted that he had also taken nude videos of about 10 other women in hotels and posted the footage online. 

Andrews has broken down into tears multiple times while taking the stand in Nashville, revealing that former employer ESPN forced her into an Oprah interview to talk about her ordeal.

According to Andrews, bosses at the sports company told her she would not be allowed back on air without opening up about her stalker. ESPN has refused to comment.

Andrews told jurors Monday that it ripped her apart when some said they thought the videos were a publicity stunt. 

She broke down, saying she's anxious and depressed, and still gets taunted online by people who've seen the videos.
