Suspect charged with killing rapper Slim Dunkin in 'row over candy' surrenders to police

By Craig Mackenzie
Updated: 08:30 EDT, 27 December 2011

View
comments

Accused: Vinson Hardimon turned himself in

A man suspected of killing rapper Slim Dunkin at a recording studio has surrendered to police, it was revealed today.

Vinson Hardimon, known as rapper Young Vito, gave himself up to Atlanta officers yesterday afternoon.

He was handcuffed and taken to  Fulton County jail where he is being held on murder and gun possession charges.

Police allege the 28-year-old gunned down Dunkin, 24, whose real name was Mario Hamilton, in a fight over a piece of candy.

Hardimon's appearance had changed dramatically since a police wanted picture was issued which showed him with a goatee beard, moustache and long hair.

'He cut off those dreadlocks and was clean-shaven,' said a detective.

Dunkin, who had appeared on a number of songs with Waka Flocka Flame, was shot in the chest last Friday night as he was preparing to record a video at a recording studio in the city.

Gunned down: Rapper Slim Dunkin was shot dead outside an Atlanta recording studio

Budding rapper: Dunkin was prominately featured on Waka's mixtapes like Twin Towers and LeBron Flocka Flame 3

Scene of the crime: Slim Dunkin was fatally shot in the chest outside a southeast Atlanta studio

He was taken to Grady Memorial Hospital where he was pronounced dead. 

Investigators said there were at least 20 people at the scene, each one with a different version of what happened. 

Police chief major keith Meadows said: 'It seems everybody witnessed something very different. We're just trying to go back and make sense of everything.'

Baffled: Police Major Keith Meadows said investigators were still trying to put the pieces of Slim Dunkin's murder together

He said as many as 20 people were inside the small office-type building at the time of the shooting, which took place around 5:30pm, but they were in different places.

He added: 'It appears the victim was scheduled to do a photo shoot,' Mr Meadows said. Before the video shoot took place, it appears the victim and suspect got involved in a verbal altercation.

'The suspect produced a weapon, discharged that handgun one time, striking the victim in the chest.'

The usual suspects: Meadows said there were as many as 20 people at the studio at the time the rapper was shot

Many fans have posted messages  on a Facebook page for the rapper.

The website AllHipHop.com last February described Slim Dunkin, a Detroit native, as an up-and-coming talent with 'a unique lyrical ability and style all his own.'

In an interview with the website, he described himself as someone 'trying to provide for his family by making something out of nothing just trying to beat the odds.'

Rest in peace: Slim Dunkin was taken to Grady Memorial Hospital, where he was pronounced dead

'I don't have amazing lyrical ability I just know how to speak on what I been through and where I came from,' he said.

Asked what to expect from him in 2011, he responded: 'Music, music, music!'

MTV.com reported that the Brick Squad Monopoly rapper had recently released a 20-track mix tape that featured Gucci Mane, Roscoe Dash and Pastor Troy.
