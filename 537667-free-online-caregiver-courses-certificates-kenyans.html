1. Open WHO

Open WHO is an online platform developed by the World Health Organization (WHO) to provide free, interactive, and accessible learning opportunities to health professionals, policymakers, and the public. It offers a range of courses and training modules, including caregiving courses.

You can get free caregiver skills training for families of children with developmental delays or disabilities on this site. The course takes approximately 8 hours, in which it is recommended to do one module after every 4-5 days. The course has 15 modules; you can complete it within three months of study.

This course benefits many Kenyans who are looking forward to a caregiving certificate for internal and global job opportunities. Through the course, you will be equipped with childcare skills that can be practised at home. Upon completion, students will be given a record of achievement when they receive at least 80% on the post-test.
