Girl found Dominic Elkins, 5, dead in ravine after he was 'beaten to death by Cody Metzker-Madsen'

'Cody had beaten Dominic with a brick and killed him': Horror moment 12-year-old girl found her foster brother, 5, dead in a ravine after he was 'bludgeoned by teen sibling'

Victim: Dominic Lloyd Lee Elkins, 5, was found at the base of a 25-foot ravine after his foster brother allegedly beat him with bricks

A 12-year-old girl from Iowa has described to police the harrowing moment when she and her twin sister discovered their 5-year-old foster brother murdered in a ravine last weekend.

A search warrant released Thursday in the case of Dominic Elkins, who was found dead near the family's rural home outside of Logan, shed light on the chaotic scene that unfolded last Saturday. 

Elkins' foster brother Cody Metzker-Madsen, 17, has been charged with first-degree murder for allegedly bludgeoning the boy to death with a brick.

Both Dominic and Metzker-Madsen lived in the home of Don and Julie Coolman along with the couple’s two biological daughters. 

According to the search documents made public this week and cited by the De Moines Register, one of girls who was left in charge of her foster brothers while the parents were away was watching TV when Metzker-Madsen come into the house with blood on his forehead, saying that something had happened to Dominic.

The 17-year-old then led the girl and other family members, who returned home by that point, to a steep ravine at the bottom of which they discovered Dominic's body lying in the mud.

The 12-year-old told investigators that she attempted to perform CPR on the toddler, but it was too late. The girl's grandfather reached the same conclusion after checking for Dominic's pulse.

Coolmans’ biological daughter, who was on baby-sitting duty that day, said in her statement to police that her older foster brother was the culprit.

In custody: 17-year-old Cody Metzker-Madsen has 'mental issues' which may be to blame as he 'was on the same level as Dominic, a 5, 6 year old'

'Cody had beaten Dominic with a brick and killed him,' she told officers.

The tragedy played out on the Coolmans' 18-acre wooded property outside of Logan while the mother, one of her daughters and a friend went into town to get some ice cream for the kids. Don Coolman was also out at the time.

Julie Coolman said in her statement to police that before she left, she saw Metzker-Madsen and Elkins playing together outside; she added that the boys seemed excited about the cold treat she had promised to bring them.

According to family members, the 17-year-old is a special needs boy who has the mental capacity of a 5-year-old.

Mrs Coolman was away for about 30 minutes. At some point during that interval, Metzker-Madsen came into the house with mud on his clothes and a small amount of blood on his face.

He addressed his 12-year-old foster sister, saying, 'it's Dominic!'

Witnesses to tragedy: Don Coolman pictured with his twin 12-year-old daughters, who discovered their foster brother dead in a ravine

Futile efforts: One of the Coolmans' twins tried to perform CPR on Dominic, but he had no pulse by the time he was discovered

The 17-year-old told the girl that his foster brother became upset over a game and hit himself with bricks before running to a ravine.

The twin girl called her mother, who by that time returned and was just at the edge of the property with her other daughter.

Julie Coolman later stated that Metzker-Madsen was crying and repeating that he hadn't done anything, and that Dominic had struck himself because he thought Cody was not playing fair.

When pressed by Julie Coolman, Cody led her and his sisters to the ravine. When the twin girls spotted Dominic lying in a creek, they let out a scream.

Upon closer examination, they noticed that the 5-year-old had gashes on his face and the back of his head. His lips had already turned blue.

One of the twins attempted to revive Cody, but to no avail. Her grandfather, Mic Coolman, determined that the 5-year-old had no pulse, telling the girl that there was nothing more she could do for him.

Game gone awry: Cody told his foster sister that Dominic became upset because the teen was not playing fair and struck himself before running into a ravine

Metzker-Madsen attended a school in Omaha for teens with behavioral issues, and Mic Coolman told The Omaha World-Herald that the boy does not understand how to relate to other people.

Dominic and Cody had lived together in the same Iowa home for only two or three weeks before the 5-year-old was found dead over Labor Day weekend.

Mental issues: Mic Coolman described Cody as a teen with the mental capacity of a 5-year-old who does not know how to relate to others

Metzker-Madsen is now being held in jail on a first-degree murder charge as local authorities continue their investigation into what prompted the attack.

Don Coolman's father Mick told local station KMTV that Metzker-Madsen's 'mental issues' may be to blame as he 'was on the same level as Dominic, a 5, 6 year old'.

'Julie loved him and had bonded with them. They need their privacy and time to grieve,' Mic Coolman said of his daughter-in-law.

She is not the only one, as Dominic's birth mother is devastated over her son's death.

Barbara Kunch, 24, spoke to The Des Moines Register in the wake of his death, telling how she had placed the boy in foster care because he had severe behavioral problems and she was unable to handle him.

'It’s not something I wanted to do, but it’s something that needed to be done,' she said of the difficult decision to put him into the state's care. 

Dominic, 5, would throw uncontrollable tantrums and physically abuse others.

That aggressive behavior had apparently come under control in recent days after he received months of therapy at a local treatment center before he was put into the Coolmans' care.

Issues: Dominic's biological mother put him into the care of the state because he had behavioral problems that she could not cope with and he received months of treatment

Better days: Dominic's biological mother, Barbara Kunch, 24, has spoken out about her devastation

'It was amazing. There’s no word to describe it,' Ms Kunch said of her son's behavioral progress.

'I can’t even look in the mirror. I just start bawling. He was my world. He really was.'

Police are still investigating what led up to the alleged altercation between Dominic and Cody.

The boy's body was found inside a ravine last Saturday evening, meaning that first responders had to repel down 20-25 feet in order to retrieve the body.

He had bruises on his head and torso.

Relatives of the foster parents say that they are in a state of shock.

'Some foster families probably are in it for the money. This family, our daughter in law and son are not in it for the money. They loved little Dominic,' Mic Coolman said.

The Coolmans, who had been foster parents for 11 years, had received training to handle children with behavioral and mental problems.
