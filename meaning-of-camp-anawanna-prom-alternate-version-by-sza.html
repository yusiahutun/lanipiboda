The lyrics take us on a journey through a nostalgic summer camp experience, but with a twist that delves into the deeper emotions and fears of never wanting to grow up. Each lyric serves as a window into a different aspect of these themes, painting a vivid picture of the struggles and desires that accompany the human experience.The opening lines, "Anawanna camp / I don't want to grow up / If I don't gotta," immediately set the tone for the song. The reference to "Anawanna camp" evokes a sense of summer nostalgia, a time when life was simpler and filled with carefree adventures. The desire to not grow up resonates with the childlike urge to hold onto joy and innocence, suggesting a resistance against facing the responsibilities and challenges that come with adulthood. It acknowledges that the journey of growing up is filled with missteps and errors, but that these moments of failure can be transformative. This highlights the importance of embracing imperfections and understanding that they play a vital role in shaping our identities.Another lyric that stands out is "Over other..."
