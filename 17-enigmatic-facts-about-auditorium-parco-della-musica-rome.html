Architectural masterpiece

The Auditorium Parco della Musica in Rome is an architectural masterpiece designed by renowned architect Renzo Piano. Its magnificent and futuristic design has made it an iconic landmark in the city.

Three concert halls

The auditorium complex consists of three concert halls, namely Sala Santa Cecilia, Sala Sinopoli, and Sala Petrassi. Each hall is designed with state-of-the-art acoustics, providing an unparalleled listening experience for music enthusiasts.

Outdoor amphitheater

In addition to the indoor concert halls, Auditorium Parco della Musica features a stunning outdoor amphitheater known as the Cavea. With a seating capacity of over 3,000, it hosts open-air performances during the summer months.

Home to Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia

The auditorium serves as the permanent home for the renowned Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia, one of the oldest musical institutions in the world. The academy regularly performs classical concerts and promotes music education.

Magnificent spiral design

The striking spiral design of the auditorium complex is inspired by ancient Roman architecture. The interconnected buildings and walkways create a visually stunning experience for visitors.

Enchanting sound quality

The concert halls at Auditorium Parco della Musica are known for their exceptional sound quality. The meticulous design and usage of advanced sound technologies ensure unparalleled acoustics, making it a favorite venue for world-class musicians.

Hosts prestigious festivals

Auditorium Parco della Musica is the stage for various prestigious festivals, including the RomaEuropa Festival and the International Piano Competition. These events attract artists and audiences from around the globe.

Underground parking facility

The complex offers convenient underground parking facilities, ensuring easy access for visitors attending concerts or other events. This feature makes it a popular choice for both locals and tourists.

Sculptures and art installations

The auditorium is adorned with sculptures and art installations, adding to its aesthetic appeal. These artworks are integrated into the architecture, creating a harmonious blend of visual and auditory experiences.

Music library and recording studios
