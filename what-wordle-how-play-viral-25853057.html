What is Wordle? How to play the viral game that's taking the world by storm

March 2024 · 4 minute read

By now almost everyone has heard of Wordle. The viral game's yellow and green grids continues to take over our Twitter timelines, and you may be wondering what exactly it is.

Wordle is the quick but challenging new word game that exploded in popularity just over the Christmas holiday period and early in the New Year.

The brain teaser, which lets you share your scores with others via social media has gotten everyone in a competitive mood as they attempt to their win streak going.

Here's what you need to know about the trending game including who created the addictive game as well as some clever tips and tricks to make sure you guess the right word every time.

What is the word game Wordle and how do you play it?

Wordle is a simple but tricky brain teaser which asks players to solve just one-word puzzle per day.

The puzzle is a five-letter word that is the same for everyone and since it changes every day you can play it daily.

All you need to do is guess the single word each time you play - you have six attempts to get it right.

The game uses a colour-coded system which gives you a little hint by letting you know whether a letter is in the right spot or not.

For example, if you guess a word incorrectly, but one of the letters is right and in the correct position, that box turns green.

If a letter is in the word but it’s not in the right place in your guess, then the box turns yellow, and if a letter is not in the word at all, the box turns grey.

The game lets you challenge yourself by turning on a 'hard mode' which forces players to use hints given by the game, stopping you from guessing words at random.

Wordle is free and can be played easily from any device that you can browse the internet including your phone, tablet or laptop. You can play the daily puzzle here.

What are the green and yellow boxes I keep seeing on Twitter?

If you've been seeing the yellow and green boxes all over Twitter, that's because the game has blown up on social media this past week.

The act of unpicking a word daily becomes quite addictive.

Since the word is the same for everyone, its also fun to compare your guesses with that of friends, family or even strangers playing online.

If you don't get the word right, it's still pretty entertaining to share your score on social media and have a laugh about it, which completely explains why Wordle is the latest viral trend that's everyone jumping on.

Tips and tricks

If you're sick of getting grey boxes with each of your guesses, these tips and tricks could be helpful:

Choosing your first word: The first word might seem like a throw-away guess, but it's actually the most important guess. So in order to make sure you get the most out of your first guess, choose carefully. Pick a word choose a word with three vowels and five different letters. Some examples that work well are orate, media, radio, adieu etc.

Avoid reusinggrays: The keyboard at the bottom of Wordle that shows what letters are green, yellow and grey. Make use of this and avoid reusing letters that already come up gray. The effort of resisting the urge to use letters that have already been tried will always pay off.

Letters can appear twice: Letters can appear twice in some words. So when you're running out of letters, don't fret. Try a word where a letter recurs. Some examples of answers from the past include chill, sissy and ferry.

Who made Wordle?

Wordle was created by software engineer Josh Wardle, who originally created the game for his partner, a fan of word games. People might remember Wardle as the creator of Place, a wild collaborative art project/social experiment from April 2017.

Place was a shared online space that allowed literally anyone to fight over what was drawn there. This ended up with huge communities battling for space on a gigantic online canvas.
