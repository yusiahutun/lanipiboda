    How to Choose the Best Rose Gold Hair Dye?

  • Composition of Ingredients
    Rose gold hair dyes should contain humectants and emollients like aloe vera, coconut oil, and argan oil that help to restore the lost moisture to the hair shafts which is lost due to regular exposure to environmental stressors. It should also be devoid of parabens, sulfates, alcohol, and ammonia as it may trigger inert scalp infections and commence the process of hair deterioration. 
  • Texture
    The texture of the rose gold hair dye should have a robust creme-based formulation as it may lead to dripping and stain clothes and cause itching redness and skin scaling. 
  • Hair and Scalp Type
    The hair and scalp type of the individual plays an important role in selecting the most compatible rose gold hair dye. While the oily and oily scalp is more susceptible to dirt and grime accumulation they will need more anti-bacterial elements like tea tree oil and lavender to prevent scalp zits. Dry hair and dry scalp will need more humectants, emollients, and occlusives like coconut oil, vitamin E, and aloe vera to protect the hair from breakage due to over-drying. 
  • Additional Tools of Application
    Hair colors should come with additional tools for application like gloves and hair caps to prevent dripping and messiness all around the place. To add on, it should also contain an ergonomically designed smooth applicator to facilitate easy application.

    How to Dye Your Hair in Rose Gold At Home?

  • Firstly, wear an old shirt, proper gloves, and an apron to prevent fading 
  • In case the product has a developer, mix the developer along with the tint to facilitate easy coloring 
  • Clip your hair into sections and divide them uniformly 
  • Begin by applying the color from the roots to the tips 
  • Let the color sit on your hair as per your waiting time 
  • Wash your hair with water or shampoo as prescribed by the brand.

    How to Maintain Rose Gold Hair Post Coloring?

  • Avoid washing your hair too often as it may weaken the color locked within the cuticles. 
  • Follow up with regular touch-ups as per the intensity and resistance of the hair tint. 
  • Make use of color-protectant shampoos and conditioners in order to prevent uneven patchy fading.
