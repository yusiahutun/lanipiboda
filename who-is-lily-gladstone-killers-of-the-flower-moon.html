Who Is Lily Gladstone, &quot;Killers of the Flower Moon&quot; Star?

April 2024 · 5 minute read

Lily Gladstone’s breakout role in Martin Scorsese’s Killers of the Flower Moon is set to make them a household name.

Gladstone—who uses she/they pronouns, according to their Instagram profile—plays Mollie Burkhart in the epic drama, adapted from journalist David Grann’s 2017 nonfiction book Killers of the Flower Moon: The Osage Murders and the Birth of the FBI. The film officially dropped last October.

Mollie, a woman from the Osage Nation, is part of a family being targeted and killed for their connection to the oil-rich lands of Oklahoma. Her husband, Ernest Burkhart (played by Leonardo DiCaprio), is part of a scheme that sees many of Mollie’s family members killed for oil rights. Other members of the star-studded ensemble include Robert De Niro, Jesse Plemons, and Brendan Fraser.

The 37-year-old actor’s performance has rightfully earned them their first-ever Golden Globe for Best Performance by an Actress in a Motion Picture, Drama, making them the first Indigenous person to receive the award. They may make history yet again this Sunday at the 96th Academy Awards, where they are nominated for Best Actress.

Ahead, we break down everything to know about the movie star.

They grew up on the Blackfeet Reservation and in Seattle.

Gladstone is of Siksikaitsitapi and NiMíiPuu heritage—their father’s background is Blackfeet and Nez Perce, while their mother is white. They grew up on the Blackfeet Reservation in northwestern Montana, living there until age 11 years. Their family eventually moved to the suburbs of Seattle due to a “lack of economic opportunities,” they told The Guardian.

They found suburban life to be alienating compared with the “mutual reliance” they had grown used to on the reservation. “Everyone seemed so isolated in their little homes,” they told the outlet. Reservation life impacted their desire to be an actor: “I am shaped by my community—and I have been supported in my ambition to be an actor and storyteller.”

Victor Boyko//Getty Images

They graduated with a BFA in acting/directing from the University of Montana.

Gladstone became the first Native American to earn a Presidential Leadership Scholarship when they enrolled at the university in 2004, according to UM’s website. There, they studied acting and pursued a minor in Native American studies before graduating with high honors in 2008, per The Guardian.

Greg Johnson, one of their instructors at the Montana Repertory Theatre, told UM, “There are a lot of talented kids at UM, but she’s always been a standout. She’s absolutely a transcendent actress. We were lucky to have her.” He added, “I think she’s going to weather the slings and arrows of the profession very well. She’s centered. She knows who she is.”

Their acting credits include Certain Women and Reservation Dogs.

They made their onscreen debut in 2013 with roles in drama films Jimmy P: Psychotherapy of a Plains Indian and Winter in the Blood. Since then, Gladstone has gone on to star opposite Kristen Stewart, Michelle Williams, and Laura Dern in the 2016 drama Certain Women and made a two-episode appearance as a grieving mother in Hulu’s Reservation Dogs.

Jim Spellman//Getty Images

Michelle Williams, Laura Dern, Lily Gladstone, and Kristen Stewart attend the Certain Women premiere in 2016.

Leonardo DiCaprio calls them the “heart and soul” of Killers of the Flower Moon.

DiCaprio gushed about his costar in a rare interview with Entertainment Tonight.

“I felt her at moments living as Mollie,” the Oscar winner told the outlet. “She embodied that character; she took on the soul of this woman and embraced herself into the Osage community.”

He added: “Once again, being from a different tribe, going to the Osage community, listening to [Mollie’s] story and direct descendants, and just embodied in every moral fiber of her being who Mollie was. [And she] expressed it through her performance in a very profound way.” He concluded, “She is the heart and soul of this movie, she really is.”

Gladstone shared a similar sentiment of their working relationship.

“The first day that I worked with him, my hands were shaking, naturally, because it’s Leonardo DiCaprio,” they told ET. “But after getting through those initial nerves, I was just sitting across from an incredibly present, generous, talented, immensely talented actor.”

Melinda Sue Gordon//Apple

They may make history in the upcoming awards circuit.

Since Killers of the Flower Moon first premiered at the Cannes Film Festival this past May, awards pundits have ranked Gladstone as a top contender for Best Actress at the Academy Awards. If nominated, they will be the fourth-ever Indigenous person nominated in the Best Actress category. If they win, they will be the first to ever receive the honor.

Besides the Oscars buzz, Gladstone has potential to make history at other awards ceremonies. Variety notes that Indigenous actresses have yet to receive a prize or a nomination from the Screen Actors Guild or Critics Choice Awards.

They were in the middle of applying for a job at the Washington State Department of Agriculture when they got cast in Killers of the Flower Moon.

Gladstone was on the verge of pausing their acting ambitions to work at the Washington State Department of Agriculture. Inspired by their passion for bees—who have been negatively impacted by Asian giant hornets, an invasive species—they began to look “into a seasonal data-analytics job” that tracked the hornets at the state department, according to Vulture. The outlet notes: “Then she got the notification requesting a Zoom with Scorsese.”
