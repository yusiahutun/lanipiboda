Summer is around the corner, and so are pool days (and if we’re lucky, a trip or two to an actual beach!). And while we firmly believe that all bodies are beach bodies and there are no such thing as “problem areas,” it’s also empowering to be able to choose what parts of your body you want to show off (or not!) in your swimsuit. Below, we’ve rounded up the best, modest swimwear, including slimming one-pieces, swim tops and bottoms (hello swim leggings!), and even less-than-revealing bikinis and tankinis.

So whatever your age and body type, we can guarantee there is a figure-flattering, modest bathing suit style you’ll actually love wearing. Bonus: the more coverage you choose, the more sun protection!

If you want to hide a tummy, shop for shapewear swimsuits with tummy control, or invisible piping that creates a Spanx-like effect on your midsection. Miraclesuits (available at Macys), are popular for their magical ability to make you look 10 pounds lighter — the style of this Miraclesuit Tramonto Belle Temptation Underwire One-Piece ($184, Macys) proves what a great cut and print can do for your waistline. You can also find flowy swim tops and tankinis (like this colorful Amazon tankini) that are less restricting but accomplish the same goal.

What is considered a modest swimsuit?

It really depends on your body type. Here’s the best modest coverage swimwear for each shape:

  • Pear shape: For those with larger legs and butts who want to conceal their lower half, look for full-coverage bottoms, swim leggings and shorts, and swim skirts that flare. And while you may not be looking to bare much cleavage, you can still consider a modest swim stop that boasts a flattering neckline to draw attention upward.
  • Apple shape: Apples, or those with larger busts and narrower hips, may want cleavage-coverage swimsuits that leave extra room for the tummy. Bold-print tops and ruffled tankinis look great on this body type!
  • Hourglass shape: Those who have curves look great in one-pieces and swim dresses that cinch at the waist, or high-waisted bikinis. Just make sure to choose a full-coverage swim top for ample cleavage control.
  • Straight shape: Straight shapes are pretty proportionate, but don’t tend to have one particular asset that “pops.” Swim skirts that flare, wrap-style one-pieces, and swimsuits with ruffles can create curves without showing too much skin.
