Early life

The speaker was born in Boston, Massachusetts, U.S.A., on January 16, 1967. He is the eldest child of Thomas W. Weeks and Leona Brown Weeks. He also has a younger brother, Abdullah Azeez. Weeks is a third-generation American preacher – his parents are also preachers, just like his grandparents.

Thomas Weeks spent much of his childhood moving around due to his father's preaching career. Besides Boston, the family also lived in New York and Indiana. They ultimately settled in Wilmington, Delaware, where his father became the Greater Bethel Apostolic Temple pastor.

Thomas Weeks attended the University of Delaware, where he pursued a degree in mass communication. However, after graduating, he opted to follow in the family's footsteps of a career in church service.

He attended the Christian International College of Theology, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Theology. Later, he returned to the same institution, pursuing a Master of Arts degree in Christian Counselling.

Pastor and preacher

After he completed his Theology course, Bishop Weeks joined the Pentecostal Assembles of the World, where he was ordained as an elder. He then started working as a preacher under his father's church, the New Destiny Fellowship. He held several roles in the church, from a church administrator to an assistant pastor and later a bishop.

After serving under his father for a while, Bishop Thomas Weeks left to form his ministry. He launched the New Destiny Christian Cathedral Church in Washington, DC, in 1997. The church (currently known as the Global Destiny Church) later evolved into a global internet community, MyGlobalDestiny.com in 2003.

In 2006, Bishop Weeks and his first wife, Juanita Bynum, opened a second church location for their ministry in Duluth, Georgia. They were planning a third location in California before their marriage fell apart. Besides the Global Destiny Church, Bishop Weeks and his second wife, Pastor Christina Glenn, also opened a second ministry, The Mega Life Church.

Wife and children

What does Bishop Thomas Weeks' family life look like? The preacher was previously married to Juanita Bynum. She is an American gospel singer, preacher, and author. They met sometime in the early 2000s during their work as preachers. The couple tied the knot in a civil ceremony in Las Vegas on July 22, 2002.

After word of their marriage got out, Weeks and Bynum announced their relationship on Trinity Broadcasting Network (T.B.N.). Then, in 2003, the couple had another public marriage ceremony, which drew the public's attention for its extravagance.

The second wedding occurred at the Regent Wall Street Hotel, New York, where about one thousand guests attended. The event was also televised live on the T.B.N. channel. During the ceremony, Bynum wore an expensive 7.76-carat diamond ring and gown with hand-sewn crystals, which took nine months to make. The wedding is reported to have cost over $1 million.

After their marriage, Weeks and Bynum started counselling other couples on marriage and love issues. Their lavish affair and seemingly perfect relationship led many, especially their congregants, to believe theirs was a union made in heaven. But unfortunately, their marriage did not last long. The couple separated around 2006 after a series of relationship issues.

The final fallout between Weeks and Bynum happened in 2007. On August 21, 2007, the couple met at a hotel in Atlanta to discuss their marital problems and reconcile. However, their discussions escalated into an argument, where Weeks allegedly choked Bynum, pushed her to the ground, and kicked her.

After the incident, the preacher fled the scene, and Bynum initially refused medical treatment and police presence. However, she later visited the hospital, where pictures of her bruises were taken and later leaked.

After the incident, Bynum filed a restraining order against her husband, who later turned himself to the authorities. Initially, he denied physically assaulting her but would later plead guilty to charges of aggravated assault. Bynum then filed for divorce, which was finalized in 2008.

After his divorce, Bishop Weeks went on a search for a new love interest and even started a reality TV show, the Next Mrs Weeks. He found love again with Christina Glenn, whom he wed – this time in private – in 2009. The couple is still married to this day.
