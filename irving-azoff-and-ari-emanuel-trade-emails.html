Irving Azoff And Ari Emanuel Trade Emails

May 2024 · 5 minute read

LOS ANGELES (CelebrityAccess MediaWire) — An apparent email dispute between William Morris Endeavor head Ari Emanuel and former Live Nation chairman Irving Azoff has gone public when the email missives were CC'd to a number of industry execs, including Cablevision's James Dolan, Live Nation CEO Michael Rapino.

As reported by Deadline Hollywood and The Hollywood Reporter, the exchange appears to have been sparked by a story in the Financial Times about the performance of Live Nation's stock since Azoff's exit in December. Emanuel is a current member of Live Nation's board of directors.

In the exchange, Emanuel prods Azoff, pointing out the FT article and inquiring about how much Azoff lost by selling his Live Nation stock before the share price improved. Additional controversy follows, with Azoff characterizing Emanuel as a "psycopath" and Emanual in turn, describing Azoff as a "horrible person."

Some of the dispute appears to stem from WME's clients leaving the firm for competitor Creative Artists Agency, where Azoff's son Jeffrey works as an agent since October 2012, and to where several of Azoff's clients have since moved their business.

Finally, in an apparent attempt to defuse the incident, Azoff's wife Shelli contacted The Hollywood Reporter to set the record straight, telling the publication that "Anybody who knows Irving and I, know that we try to live our lives in a fair and honorable way," she says. "I think that all of our emails to Ari show that.. I wish Ari success and happiness, now and always."
